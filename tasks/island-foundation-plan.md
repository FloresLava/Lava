# Island Foundation Implementation Plan

## Objective

Plan the large-scale environmental structure, traversal flow, spatial readability, and emotional pacing of the island before terrain production begins.

This phase focuses ONLY on:
- terrain planning
- traversal structure
- navigation flow
- environmental pacing
- landmark hierarchy
- regional transitions
- visibility planning
- traversal psychology

No gameplay systems should be implemented during this phase.

---

## Core Planning Philosophy

The island should feel:
- ancient
- natural
- asymmetrical
- readable
- emotionally navigable

Players should:
- explore outward from the hub
- experience controlled environmental tension
- periodically regain orientation
- naturally reconnect with the central tree

The island should support:
- curiosity
- discovery
- recovery
- return flow

---

## Island Shape Strategy

The island silhouette should:
- feel visually memorable
- avoid procedural symmetry
- support recognizable regional identities
- remain readable from both ground level and overhead views

Planned structure:
- central elevated hub region
- uneven outer coastline
- distinct regional silhouettes
- natural inward flow toward the tree

Avoid:
- perfect circular layouts
- excessive coastline emptiness
- disconnected biome shapes
- overly fragmented landmass silhouettes

---

## Central Tree Placement Strategy

The massive tree functions as:
- the island's dominant landmark
- the primary navigation anchor
- the emotional safe hub
- the spatial center of traversal flow

The tree should:
- remain visually reconnectable from most regions
- feel ancient and permanent
- create emotional stability for players
- visually overpower secondary landmarks

Planned terrain around the tree:
- gentle slopes
- calm traversal flow
- exposed root formations
- open navigable terrain
- partial sightlines into surrounding regions

Avoid:
- excessive terrain obstruction near the hub
- competing landmark dominance
- visually noisy terrain surrounding the tree

---

## Traversal Flow Strategy

Primary traversal rhythm:

1. Player departs from central hub
2. Player enters regional biome
3. Environmental tension/disorientation increases
4. Landmarks or sightlines restore orientation
5. Player naturally reconnects toward the hub

Traversal goals:
- encourage exploration
- maintain movement readability
- avoid hard dead ends
- support environmental discovery
- create outward-and-return traversal loops

Traversal routes should:
- branch naturally into secondary exploration paths
- preserve readable primary flow
- avoid maze-like structure
- support smooth terrain movement

---

## Regional Transition Strategy

Regional transitions should feel gradual and environmentally believable.

### North → West

Transition goals:
- cliffs gradually fracture into ruin terrain
- exposed roots increase in density
- terrain becomes heavier and more fragmented

---

### West → South

Transition goals:
- ruins become consumed by overgrowth
- visibility decreases gradually
- foliage density increases naturally

---

### South → East

Transition goals:
- dense foliage slowly opens
- terrain becomes calmer and more breathable
- shoreline visibility gradually increases

---

### East → North

Transition goals:
- shoreline terrain becomes rougher
- elevation gradually increases
- terrain exposure becomes more severe

Avoid:
- abrupt biome boundaries
- sudden density shifts
- unnatural environmental separation

---

## Sightline and Landmark Strategy

Players should periodically:
- regain visual connection to the central tree
- identify recognizable landmarks
- naturally recover orientation

Visibility variation by region:

East:
- highest visibility
- open sightlines
- long shoreline readability

South:
- lowest visibility
- enclosed traversal
- controlled environmental compression

North:
- elevated long-distance visibility
- exposed terrain readability

West:
- interrupted visibility through ruins and roots
- fragmented visual structure

Avoid:
- constant full visibility
- complete navigational blindness
- excessive landmark competition

---

## Scale and Traversal Planning

Target traversal pacing:

- Hub crossing:
  short and immediately readable

- Regional traversal:
  moderate exploration duration

- Full island loop:
  meaningful without becoming exhausting

Scale goals:
- regions feel distinct
- traversal remains engaging
- terrain scale feels intentional
- players rarely experience traversal fatigue

Avoid:
- oversized empty terrain
- excessive traversal downtime
- cramped movement spaces without purpose

---

## Terrain Density Strategy

High density:
- South foliage zones
- West ruins/root formations

Medium density:
- North cliffs/root terrain

Low density:
- East shoreline
- Central hub region

Density variation should support:
- emotional pacing
- traversal readability
- environmental identity
- visibility control

---

## Environmental Atmosphere Goals

Center:
- safe
- grounded
- spiritually ancient

North:
- exposed
- elevated
- weathered

East:
- open
- breathable
- calm

South:
- enclosed
- tense
- overgrown

West:
- ancient
- heavy
- reclaimed by nature

Players should emotionally feel environmental shifts while traversing between regions.

---

## Risk Assessment

Potential risks:
- regions blending visually
- oversized terrain scale
- environmental clutter
- navigation confusion
- loss of landmark readability
- excessive landmark competition
- traversal fatigue

Mitigation strategies:
- preserve landmark hierarchy
- maintain regional contrast
- preserve central tree visibility rhythm
- maintain readable traversal flow
- control terrain density intentionally

---

## Execution Readiness

Terrain production may begin after human approval.
