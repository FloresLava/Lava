# Island Foundation Task

## Objective

Create the foundational terrain layout for the island world.

Focus ONLY on:
- island silhouette
- terrain shape language
- regional identity
- navigational readability
- traversal flow
- environmental atmosphere
- hub-centered world structure

Do NOT implement gameplay systems during this phase.

---

## Core World Structure

The island centers around a massive ancient tree visible from most major regions.

The tree acts as:
- the island's visual anchor
- the primary navigation landmark
- the emotional safe hub
- the central orientation point between regions

The tree should:
- feel ancient and dominant
- naturally guide player orientation
- create a sense of safety and return
- remain visually reconnectable from most areas

---

## Central Hub Requirements

The center region functions as the island's primary safe hub.

Requirements:
- large open clearing
- calm readable terrain
- smooth movement flow
- partial visual connection toward surrounding regions
- clear exits toward north, east, south, and west regions
- reserved space for future hub systems
- avoid cluttered terrain
- avoid dense visual obstruction

Players should feel relief when returning to the center area.

The hub should emotionally contrast surrounding regions by feeling:
- safe
- grounded
- open
- stable
- ancient
- welcoming

---

## Scale and Traversal Requirements

- Island scale must feel intentional and playable
- Traversal should feel smooth and readable
- Avoid oversized empty terrain
- Avoid cramped movement spaces unless regionally intentional
- Terrain should encourage natural exploration loops
- Traversal routes should create a natural outward-and-return flow centered around the hub
- Main routes should flow naturally between regions
- Players may experience brief disorientation in some regions, but should be able to naturally recover orientation using landmarks
- Terrain elevation should feel traversable
- Avoid maze-like layouts
- Preserve movement readability at all times

---

## Required Regions

### North Region

Theme:
Elevated cliffs and exposed terrain.

Requirements:
- strong elevation changes
- cliffside terrain
- exposed roots gripping stone
- windy/open atmosphere
- sparse vegetation
- long visibility distances

---

### East Region

Theme:
Calm shoreline and softer terrain.

Requirements:
- smooth shoreline curves
- soft elevation transitions
- open visibility
- breathable/open atmosphere
- beginner-friendly terrain flow
- relaxed traversal spaces

---

### South Region

Theme:
Dense enclosed foliage region.

Requirements:
- enclosed terrain flow
- curved pathways
- denser terrain spacing
- reduced visibility
- jungle-like atmosphere
- stronger environmental tension

---

### West Region

Theme:
Overgrown ruins reclaimed by nature.

Requirements:
- uneven terrain
- broken land formations
- reserved ruin spaces
- thicker root presence
- heavier environmental atmosphere
- visually ancient terrain identity

---

## Terrain Constraints

- Preserve strong island silhouette readability
- Regions must transition naturally
- Regions should feel distinct without feeling disconnected
- Avoid abrupt biome transitions unless intentionally dramatic
- Preserve clear sightline breaks between regions
- Maintain directional awareness
- Encourage player reorientation using the central tree
- Avoid excessive terrain noise
- Avoid over-detailing terrain
- Avoid visual clutter
- Preserve long-distance landmark readability

---

## Visual Readability Goals

Players should:
- immediately recognize regional differences
- maintain directional awareness
- identify major landmarks from distance
- understand major traversal routes naturally
- visually reconnect with the central tree frequently
- emotionally feel regional transitions while traversing

---

## Forbidden Behaviors

Agents must NOT:
- add enemies
- add combat systems
- add weather systems
- add celestial systems
- add underground systems
- create unrelated buildings
- add excessive props
- over-detail terrain
- introduce lore systems
- create puzzle systems

---

## Deliverables

Agents must provide:

1. Terrain summary
2. Region breakdown
3. Traversal observations
4. Navigation/readability observations
5. Scale concerns
6. Suggested future expansion spaces

---

## Completion Criteria

Task is complete when:
- island silhouette feels readable
- traversal feels smooth
- scale feels intentional
- all regions feel visually distinct
- players naturally orient using the central tree
- the central hub feels safe and readable
- traversal supports outward exploration and return to hub
- players emotionally feel regional transitions while traversing
- no unrelated systems were introduced
