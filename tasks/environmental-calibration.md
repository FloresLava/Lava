# Environmental Calibration Task

## Objective

Create a small environmental prototype used to calibrate:
- world scale
- traversal feel
- environmental readability
- landmark visibility
- terrain density
- atmosphere pacing
- player-relative proportions

This phase exists to establish the island’s environmental language before large-scale terrain production begins.

No gameplay systems should be implemented during this phase.

---

## Core Calibration Philosophy

The purpose of this prototype is NOT to:
- finish the island
- build full terrain
- create gameplay content
- detail every environment

The purpose is to:
- establish scale language
- establish traversal rhythm
- establish emotional pacing
- establish environmental readability
- establish landmark relationships
- define player-world proportion

This prototype acts as the environmental reference standard for future terrain production.

---

## Prototype Requirements

The calibration area should include:

- one central tree prototype
- one open clearing
- one root-based traversal path
- one elevation transition
- one partial shoreline view
- one controlled visibility break
- one environmental overlook point
- one intentional landmark framing moment
- one small transition into denser terrain

The area should remain small, focused, and intentionally readable.

Avoid:
- excessive world scale
- unnecessary decoration
- large empty terrain
- unrelated structures
- disconnected environmental pieces

---

## Player Scale Calibration

The environment should be built relative to the player's emotional perception of scale.

Goals:
- the tree should feel ancient and dominant
- traversal should feel readable and intentional
- cliffs should feel elevated without becoming exhausting
- open areas should feel breathable without feeling empty
- enclosed areas should feel tense without feeling cramped

Avoid:
- toy-like environmental proportions
- oversized traversal distances
- excessive environmental compression
- unrealistic environmental scale inflation

---

## Traversal Calibration Goals

Players should:
- naturally understand where to move
- feel invited toward traversal paths
- periodically rediscover landmarks
- experience mild environmental tension
- recover orientation naturally

Traversal rhythm should alternate between:
- openness
- enclosure
- visibility
- compression
- recovery

Traversal should create occasional moments of anticipation before environmental reveal.

Avoid:
- nonstop visual density
- uninterrupted openness
- maze-like traversal
- constant environmental noise

---

## Visibility and Landmark Calibration

The central tree should:
- dominate environmental hierarchy
- remain emotionally important
- periodically reappear during traversal
- stabilize player orientation

Visibility testing should include:
- partial obstruction
- landmark rediscovery
- framed sightlines
- elevated viewpoints
- controlled visibility loss

Avoid:
- constant full visibility
- complete visual disconnection
- excessive landmark competition

---

## Terrain Density Calibration

Test varying environmental density levels:

Low density:
- open clearing
- shoreline visibility

Medium density:
- roots
- uneven terrain
- elevation transitions

High density:
- enclosed foliage
- visibility compression
- denser traversal spaces

Density should support:
- pacing
- atmosphere
- readability
- emotional variation

Avoid:
- repetitive terrain compositions
- clutter-heavy environments
- visual noise saturation

---

## Atmosphere Calibration Goals

The environment should feel:
- ancient
- calm
- mysterious
- breathable
- emotionally grounded

The environment should feel stylized and emotionally readable rather than hyper-realistic.

The player should feel:
- curiosity
- comfort near the tree
- mild uncertainty during exploration
- relief when regaining orientation

Avoid:
- excessive darkness
- horror-focused atmosphere
- hyper-realistic visual noise
- overwhelming environmental intensity

---

## Style Reference Direction

Environmental inspiration includes:
- The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time
- Shadow of the Colossus
- The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker
- Studio Ghibli environmental pacing

Focus on:
- readability
- emotional scale
- landmark clarity
- environmental pacing
- stylized atmosphere

Do NOT directly copy existing environments.

---

## Deliverables

Agents must provide:

1. Scale observations
2. Traversal observations
3. Landmark readability observations
4. Density observations
5. Visibility observations
6. Environmental pacing observations
7. Known scale/readability concerns

---

## Completion Criteria

Calibration is successful when:
- the world scale feels emotionally coherent
- traversal feels readable and intentional
- the central tree feels dominant and stabilizing
- environmental pacing feels natural
- visibility rhythm supports exploration
- density variation feels emotionally effective
- the prototype establishes a clear environmental language
